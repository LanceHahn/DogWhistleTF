This week, Congress passed the Emmett Till Anti-Lynching Act to make lynching a federal crime. It’s named for Emmett Till, a Chicago teenager who was brutally killed in Mississippi in 1955. On today’s episode of A Word, Jason Johnson is joined by historian Lopez Matthews Jr. to discuss the harmful myths about lynching, and how its specter haunts Black Americans to this day.Guest: Lopez Matthews Jr. is an executive council member for the Association for the Study of African American Life and History.Podcast production by Jasmine Ellis.
